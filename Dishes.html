Adobong Baboy

Filipino Adobong Baboy is a favorite dish of Filipinos, and due to its high popularity, many consider it to be the national dish of the Philippines. Adobo dishes can be made with pork, chicken, beef, or a combination of the two.

Beef Caldereta

Caldereta beef is the mainstay of any Filipino dish. It is a type of beef stew cooked with tomato sauce and liver. Lots of potatoes, peppers, peas and green olives.

Beef Pares

Pares, also known as beef pares, is a term for a Filipino-style braised beef dish with garlic fried rice and a bowl of clear soup. It is a popular food item especially associated with roadside catering establishments called Pares Houses that specialize in this type of meal.

Bistek

Bistek Tagalog is thinly sliced tenderloin stewed with citrus, soy sauce, onion and garlic. This Filipino beef steak is hearty and delicious and goes perfectly with steamed rice.

Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a popular Filipino dish with coconut milk, shrimp paste and slices of pork cooked with chili peppers. The perfect meal for a spicy lover.

Bopis

Bopis is a spicy Filipino delicacy made from minced pork lungs and heart cooked with onions, garlic and chili peppers. It is popular as a starter and is often used as a main course.

Bulalo

Bulalo is a light-colored soup made by boiling beef shank and bone marrow until the collagen and fat in the clear soup dissolve. It usually contains leafy vegetables, corn onions, green onions, onions, garlic, ginger and fish sauce.

Chicken Afritada

Chicken Afritada Recipe is another favorite dish in every Filipino home. Chicken is cooked with tomato sauce, bell peppers, potatoes, and carrots. You might have tried this at Fiesta, birthdays, and other popular Filipino celebrations.

Chicken Asado

Chicken Asado (also called Asadong Manok) is a spicy Filipino chicken stew cooked with potato and tomato sauce. A wonderful combination of spicy, sweet, sour and savory.

Chicken Curry

This chicken curry is cooked in Filipino style. A chicken dish cooked with potatoes, capsicum (bell peppers), carrots and coconut milk that makes the sauce rich and savory. It goes well with warm white rice.
