Puto

History

Puto is made out of slightly fermented rice called galapong, it's a steamed rice cake. Puto is also an umbrella term for various kinds of indigenous steamed cakes, including those made without rice. It is a sub-type of kakanin (rice cakes), sometimes puto is eaten with an accompaniment of different dishes. Puto has a variety of flavors, there is puto topped with cheese and also pork inside puto. Its shape is commonly round and cooked in a soldered sheet metal built around a perforated pan steamer or thin strips of bent bamboo enclosing a flat basket of split bamboo slats. The texture of puto shouldnt be sticky or dry and crumbly, but soft, moist, and with a fine, uniform grain.
