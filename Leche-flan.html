Leche-Flan

≪




Source:https://www.lutongbahayrecipe.com/desserts/leche-flan/








History

Leche-flan was brought over by Spaniards in the Philippines during the Spanish colonization. Leche-flan is a caramel custard consisting of milk, sugar, and eggs, with the addition of vanilla flavoring, traditionally it is oval-shaped due to the use of tin molds called llaneras. Its recommended to put it to chill so the syrup will be coated with the delicacy. It can be added to different kinds of desserts like halo-halo.

Make One

Ingredients

By lutongbahayrecipe.com.
  • 10 pieces raw eggs
  • 1 small can condensed milk
  • 1 cup fresh milk (or evaporated milk)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Procedure

  • Remove the yolk from the egg white (only egg yolks will be used).
  • Beat them using a fork or an egg beater.
  • Add the condensed milk and mix thoroughly.
  • Pour-in the fresh milk and vanilla. Mix well.
  • Put the mold (llanera) on top of the stove and heat using low fire.
  • Put the granulated sugar into the mold and mix thoroughly until the solid sugar turns into liquid (caramel) having a light brown color.
  • Spread the caramel (liquid sugar) evenly on the flat side of the mold.
  • Wait for 5 minutes, then pour the egg yolk and milk mixture on the mold.
  • Cover the top of the mold using an aluminum foil.
  • Steam the mold with egg and milk mixture for 30 to 35 minutes.
  • Refrigerate when the temperature cool down after steaming.
  • Serve for dessert.
