Beef Dishes

Beef Caldereta

Caldereta beef is the mainstay of any Filipino dish. It is a type of beef stew cooked with tomato sauce and liver. Lots of potatoes, peppers, peas and green olives.

Beef Pares

Pares, also known as beef pares, is a term for a Filipino-style braised beef dish with garlic fried rice and a bowl of clear soup. It is a popular food item especially associated with roadside catering establishments called Pares Houses that specialize in this type of meal.

Bistek

Bistek Tagalog is thinly sliced tenderloin stewed with citrus, soy sauce, onion and garlic. This Filipino beef steak is hearty and delicious and goes perfectly with steamed rice.

Bulalo

Bulalo is a light-colored soup made by boiling beef shank and bone marrow until the collagen and fat in the clear soup dissolve. It usually contains leafy vegetables, corn onions, green onions, onions, garlic, ginger and fish sauce.

Kare-kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino stew with a thick and savory peanut sauce. It is usually made from oxtail stew, beef offal, pork leg, veal leg, pork leg or foot, a variety of pork, beef stew, and sometimes offal.

Mechado

In general, Mechado is a stew made from tomatoes. Meats such as pork and beef are used to make this dish. It is similar to Kaldereta or Afritada because of the basic ingredients used.

Papaitan

Papaitan is a famous Ilocano soup dish consisting mainly of cattle or goat intestines. The bitter taste of this soup is due to the bile. Bitter juice extracted from the liver and stored in the gallbladder to aid digestion.

Tapa

Tapa is dried or salted beef, lamb, venison, or horse meat, although other meats or even fish may be used. Filipinos prepare tapas by using thin slices of meat and then seasoning it with salt and spices as a method of preservation. It can be fried or grilled.
