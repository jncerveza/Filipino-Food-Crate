Lengua De Gato

History

Lengua de Gato means Cats tongue in Spanish because it is thin like a cat’s tongue and the Filipino famous butter cookie. Lengua de Gato is also a crunchy cookie, placed in a small container, and a famous pasalubong you can buy in the summer capital of the Philippines, Baguio City.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 cup all purpose flour



Procedure

  1. Preheat oven to 350 F.

  2. ​Using a whisk or hand mixer, cream the butter.

  3. Gradually add sugar and continue beating until light and fluffy.

  4. Add egg whites and vanilla. Beat until fully incorporated.

  5. Gradually add the flour. Beat on low speed until well combined.

  6. Transfer to a piping bag with a round piping tip. You can also use a zip bag with one corner or tip trimmed to make a hole.

  7. Prepare a tray lined with parchment paper or silicone mat. Pipe the batter about 2 inches long with a space in between cookies.

  8. Bake for 10-13 minutes or until cookies are dry and sides starts to brown slightly.

  9. Remove from oven and transfer the cookies on a wire rack to cool completely. Recipe yields about 50 pieces.

  10. Store in an airtight container.

  11. Enjoy.
