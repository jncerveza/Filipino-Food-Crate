Ginisang Munggo

Ginisang Munggo or sauteed green beans with spinach is a simple yet flavorful Filipino vegetable dish. This dish is usually eaten as a main course with warm white rice. Some versions use fish, while others use thinly sliced pork.

Inasal na Manok

Inasal na Manok is a unique Filipino grilled chicken dish that originated in Bacolod and has become a staple of the entire Visayas region. We use a variety of chicken marinated in a mixture of vinegar and many spices such as lemongrass, garlic and ginger.

Inihaw na Bangus

Inihaw na Bangus means grilled milk fish. It is a very delicious tangy dish that anyone can easily prepare. It pairs well with soy sauce dip and rice. It is stuffed with onions, tomatoes, ginger, salt and pepper.

Kare-kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino stew with a thick and savory peanut sauce. It is usually made from oxtail stew, beef offal, pork leg, veal leg, pork leg or foot, a variety of pork, beef stew, and sometimes offal.

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is 'cooked' without the application of heat but is rather cured in vinegar. Kinilaw recipes are easy to prepare and are typically used for casual encounters. It is a popular "pulutan" or food served during casual drinking. But it also makes a great snack.

Laing

Laing is a Filipino dish made with minced or whole taro leaf meat or seafood in thick coconut milk and seasoned with labuyo chili, lemongrass, garlic, shallots, ginger and shrimp paste.

Laswa

Laswa is a vegetable Ilongo soup similar to Dinengdeng from Ilocos. The latter is seasoned with bagoong, while Laswa is usually seasoned with salt. Patola, eggplant, sigarillyas, long beans, banana heart, okra, kalabasa, malunggay, and saluyot are just a few of the many vegetables available.

Lechon

An indispensable Filipino delicacy, lechong baboy is a pork or whole pig stuffed, glazed and slowly grilled over charcoal. The most coveted part is the crunchy golden brown skin covering the tender meat that melts in your mouth.

Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai is a Filipino spring roll made from minced meat wrapped in a thin crepe called a Lumpia wrapper. The meat-filled Lumpia is rolled into a thin cylinder shape, cut to bite-sized lengths, and fried completely to a golden color.

Mechado

In general, Mechado is a stew made from tomatoes. Meats such as pork and beef are used to make this dish. It is similar to Kaldereta or Afritada because of the basic ingredients used.
