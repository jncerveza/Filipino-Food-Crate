Pastillas

History

Pastillas originated from San Miguel, Bulacan, Pastillas is a milk candy. It's covered in sugar and the container is a plastic wrap. There is also a festival being held about this candy called Pastillas Festival where they showcase their yummy milk candies wrapped in Pabalat, pabalat are only made for special occasions and orders. Pastillas are also home made by carabao-rearing farmers.


https://www.kawalingpinoy.com/pastillas-de-leche/

Ingredients

  • 5 cups fresh milk (carabao or whole cow's milk)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon lime or calamansi zestr
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3/4 cup full cream powdered milk

Procedure

  1. In a wide, nonstick skillet over low heat, combine milk and 2/3 cup of the sugar, and stir until sugar is dissolved.

  2. Cook, regularly stirring and scraping sides and bottom of the pan, for about 1 to 1 1/2 hours or until milk is reduced and mixture turns into a thin paste.

  3. Add lime zest and stir to combine.

  4. In a wide, nonstick skillet over low heat, combine milk and 2/3 cup of the sugar, and stir until sugar is dissolved.

  5. Continue to cook for about 3 to 5 minutes or until the mixture begins to pull from sides of the pan and turns into a soft dough.

  6. Transfer mixture into a lightly oiled plate and allow to cool to touch.
