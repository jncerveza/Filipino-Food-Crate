Pork Dishes

Adobong Baboy

Filipino Adobong Baboy is a favorite dish of Filipinos, and due to its high popularity, many consider it to be the national dish of the Philippines. Adobo dishes can be made with pork, chicken, beef, or a combination of the two.

Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a popular Filipino dish with coconut milk, shrimp paste and slices of pork cooked with chili peppers. The perfect meal for a spicy lover.

Bopis

Bopis is a spicy Filipino delicacy made from minced pork lungs and heart cooked with onions, garlic and chili peppers. It is popular as a starter and is often used as a main course.

Crispy Pata

Crispy Pata is a snack for pork lovers. It has crispy pork skin filled with spicy and tender meat. Crispy Pata is commonly defined as fried pork ribs or shanks, but are actually cut from the hock to the feet.

Dinuguan

Dinuguan, also known as chocolate meat or blood pudding, is a hearty dish made from cubic pork, pork blood and spices. This classic Filipino pork stew is generous and tasty, and is delicious as a main course of rice or as a mid day snack with puto.

Giniling

Pork Giniling is a dish that uses ground pork as its main ingredient. It's also one of the easiest recipes to put together. All you need is minced meat and some vegetables (potatoes, carrots, green peas).

Lechon

An indispensable Filipino delicacy, lechong baboy is a pork or whole pig stuffed, glazed and slowly grilled over charcoal. The most coveted part is the crunchy golden brown skin covering the tender meat that melts in your mouth.

Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai is a Filipino spring roll made from minced meat wrapped in a thin crepe called a Lumpia wrapper. The meat-filled Lumpia is rolled into a thin cylinder shape, cut to bite-sized lengths, and fried completely to a golden color.

Pochero

Pochero is another great dish we inherited from the Spaniards. A typical Filipino stew made with pork belly, saba banana, beans and vegetables.

Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is Filipino's favorite pork dish cooked in sour tamarind soup. It is the ultimate comfort dish. Sinigang has been and will always be a staple of Filipino cuisine across the country.

Sisig

Sisig is a Kapampangan delicacy usually served as an appetizer, but over the years has grown into a popular dinner dish. It is made from parts of a pig's face and belly, and chicken liver which is usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers.
