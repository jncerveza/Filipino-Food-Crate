Inihaw na Bangus

History

Milkfish or Bangus farming first appeared in the Philippines about 1800 years ago and spread to Indonesia, Taiwan and the Pacific Ocean. Traditional milkfish farming relied on replenishing ponds by collecting wild fry. This caused large variations in quality and quantity depending on the season and region.

Ingredients

  • 1 piece milk fish bangus, with scales but guts removed
  • 1 piece tomato ripe, diced
  • 1 piece red onion diced
  • 1 piece lemon or 3 pieces calamansi (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger minced
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Procedure

  1. Wash the milk fish. Pat it dry using a paper towel.

  2. Open the incision and then rub the salt on the inside of the dish. The fish should have an incision either above or below the belly area.

  3. Meanwhile, combine tomato, onion, and ginger in a large bowl. Squeeze some lemon juice in and add the ground black pepper. Gently stir.

  4. Stuff the mixture inside the milk fish.

  5. Grill the fish in medium heat for about 10 to 12 minutes per side.

  6. Serve with toyomansi (soy sauce with calamansi) and steamed rice.

  7. Share and enjoy!
