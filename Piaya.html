Piaya

History

Piaya is a muscovado-filled unleavened flatbread. It has a sweet taste and can be a snack or a dessert for a day. Piaya is a delicacy and started in negros occidental, Philippines, which is the sugar capital of the Philippines. And the owner of piaya, also known as Bongbong’s Piaya, is Reynaldo B. Villan or known as “Bongbong.”

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all – purpose flour
  • 10 tbsp. oil
  • ½ cup muscovado sugar
  • 4 tbsp. water
  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 8 tbsps. Oil
  • 7 tbsps. Water
  • ½ cup sesame seeds

Procedure

    FIRST PORTION MIXTURE:

  1. Mix half of the flour and half of the oil: combine them well using a large bowl.

  2. Slice it into 20 equal sizes and set aside.



    3. FOR THE FILLING:

  3. Pour a little water to the muscovado sugar to make it moist. Divide it also into 20 portions, set aside.



    4. SECOND PORTION MIXTURE:

  4. Mix the remaining flour, oil and some water. Knead to a cylinder and divide into 20 as portions as well.

  5. Flatten each portion and top with the First Portion Mixture. Roll out and stuff with muscovado filling. Close the edges.

  6. Sprinkle with sesame seeds.

  7. Bake in a preheat oven in medium heat or grill until brown on the outside. Serve it warm and fresh. Enjoy!
