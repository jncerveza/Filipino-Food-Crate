Buko Pandan

≪




Source:








History

Here's another dessert that is perfectly fit for a birthday party. Buko Pandan is a green jelly with coconut strips that is submerged under creamy milk. Theories say that buko pandan originated from Bohol. Just like other Filipino desserts, you can add different ingredients that you like. But Buko Pandan usually contains pinipig (a flattened rice dish) on top or some jelly pearls or tapioca balls.

Make One

Ingredients

This is how you make a buko pandan ( By Xtian Mack of vigattintourism.com)
  • 8 leaves of pandan, cleansed well
  • 5 buko which is no too hard and not to soft- grated in strips
  • 10 cups of buko water (water from the 5 buko)
  • 3 small cans of Nestle Cream
  • 1 medium can of condensed milk
  • 2 bars of green gulaman
  • 3/4 cup sugar

Procedure

  • First, boil the buko water with eight pandan leaves that are individually twisted. This is to break the fibers and expose the juice.
  • Let it simmer for about 20 minutes.
  • Remove the pandan leaves and make sure that the remaining coconut water is eight cups before adding the two bars of gulaman. This is because one bar of gulaman needs four cups of water. So if it is more than eight cups, the gulaman will be mushy and soft, if the water is less, the gulaman will be hard.
  • Dissolve the gulaman well by continuous stirring.
  • Add sugar while mixing. Do this for 5 minutes.
  • Pour through a strainer into cooling trays and wait till it hardens.
  • Then put it in the fridge.
  • Mix the grated buko with the 3 cans of cream and condensed milk.
  • Get the gulaman in the fridge and cut into cubes. Mix with the buko mixture.
