Sisig

Sisig is a Kapampangan delicacy usually served as an appetizer, but over the years has grown into a popular dinner dish. It is made from parts of a pig's face and belly, and chicken liver which is usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers.

Tapa

Tapa is dried or salted beef, lamb, venison, or horse meat, although other meats or even fish may be used. Filipinos prepare tapas by using thin slices of meat and then seasoning it with salt and spices as a method of preservation. It can be fried or grilled.

Tinolang Manok

Tinolang Manok is a light and refreshing chicken soup. Using chicken, spinach and green papaya, this clean, clear soup is a popular home-cooked dish in the Philippines. It is great for cool days.

Tortang Talong

Tortang talong, also known as eggplant omelette, is a fritter in Filipino cuisine, made by frying whole grilled eggplants dipped in an egg mixture.

Tuyo

Tuyo fish is a type of dried fish from the Philippines. This dish is usually fried quickly and served with rice and spicy vinegar dip. Tuyo is usually eaten for breakfast in the Philippines, although this dried fish can also be eaten at any time of the day.

Ukoy

Ukoy is the Filipino version of shrimp fritters. Small shrimp with head and shell are mixed into batter and fried until crispy. It can be an appetizer, main course or mid-afternoon snack.
