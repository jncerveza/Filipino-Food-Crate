History

Kalabasa sa gata, or ginataang kalabasa, is a Filipino vegetable stew cooked with kalabasa, coconut milk, and spices. Shrimp and yardlong beans are usually used, as well as bagoong (fermented fish or shrimp) or patis (fish sauce). It can also be made with a number of different items, such as fish, crab, or beef. It's a creamy, umami-rich dish with a touch sweetness from the calabaza. It's a ginataan variety.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs. squash cubed
  • 18 pieces string beans cut in 2-inch pieces
  • 1 piece Knorr Pork cube
  • 3 ounces pork sliced into small pieces, preferably boiled
  • 4 cups coconut milk
  • 1 piece onion chopped
  • 2 thumbs ginger sliced into strips
  • 5 cloves garlic crushed
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Fish sauce to taste

Procedure

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add pork. Cook until brown and crispy.

  2. Saute garlic, onion, and ginger.

  3. Once the onion softens, pour the coconut milk into the pan. Stir. Cover the pan and let the liquid boil. Once boiling, adjust the heat to medium and continue boiling for 5 minutes.

  4. Add Knorr Pork Cube and squash. Continue cooking for 5 minutes.

  5. Add string beans. Cook for 5 to 8 minutes.

  6. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with warm rice.

  7. Share and Enjoy!
