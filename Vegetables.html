Vegetables Dishes

Chop Suey

Chop Suey's recipe features a multitude of vegetables and meats. It's easy to saute vegetables in a thick sauce, ideal for special occasions or for a healthy everyday meal.

Ginataang Kalabasa

Ginataang Kalabasa is a vegetable stew that is popular in the Filipino culture. It is made with these two main ingredients, Kalabasa also known as Kabocha squash and coconut milk. It usually includes shrimp and yardlong beans and bagoong or patis. It can also be cooked with fish, crab or meat and a variety of other ingredients.

Ginisang Ampalaya

Ginisang Ampalaya is a delicious vegetable stir-fry made with bitter melon, tomatoes, and eggs. It's delicious, nutritious and budget friendly.

Ginisang Munggo

Ginisang Munggo or sauteed green beans with spinach is a simple yet flavorful Filipino vegetable dish. This dish is usually eaten as a main course with warm white rice. Some versions use fish, while others use thinly sliced pork.

Laing

Laing is a Filipino dish made with minced or whole taro leaf meat or seafood in thick coconut milk and seasoned with labuyo chili, lemongrass, garlic, shallots, ginger and shrimp paste.

Laswa

Laswa is a vegetable Ilongo soup similar to Dinengdeng from Ilocos. The latter is seasoned with bagoong, while Laswa is usually seasoned with salt. Patola, eggplant, sigarillyas, long beans, banana heart, okra, kalabasa, malunggay, and saluyot are just a few of the many vegetables available.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet or Pakbet is a Filipino vegetable dish that is delicious because it has many colors. A blend of local vegetables such as squash, eggplant, okra, green beans and bitter melon. Pork, shrimp paste and tomatoes are used to give it its distinctive flavor.

Tortang Talong

Tortang talong, also known as eggplant omelette, is a fritter in Filipino cuisine, made by frying whole grilled eggplants dipped in an egg mixture.
