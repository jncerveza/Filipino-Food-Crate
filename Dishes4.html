Paksiw

Paksiw is a Filipino style dish that means "cooked in vinegar and stewed". Paksiw na Isda is where the fish is boiled in vinegar and seasoned with fish sauce and chili.

Papaitan

Papaitan is a famous Ilocano soup dish consisting mainly of cattle or goat intestines. The bitter taste of this soup is due to the bile. Bitter juice extracted from the liver and stored in the gallbladder to aid digestion.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet or Pakbet is a Filipino vegetable dish that is delicious because it has many colors. A blend of local vegetables such as squash, eggplant, okra, green beans and bitter melon. Pork, shrimp paste and tomatoes are used to give it its distinctive flavor.

Pininyahang Manok

Popularly anglicized as pineapple chicken, Pininyahang Manok is a Filipino dish consisting of chicken stewed in pineapple, carrot, potato, pepper-based milk or coconut milk-based sauce. Some variations of the dish use chicken broth instead of milk.

Pochero

Pochero is another great dish we inherited from the Spaniards. A typical Filipino stew made with pork belly, saba banana, beans and vegetables.

Sarciado

Sarciadong Isda or Fish Sarciado translates as "Fish Sauce". Fried fish cooked in a sauce that usually includes tomatoes and onions; Some spices are also added to enhance the taste.

Sinabawang Gulay

Sinabawang gulay, commonly abbreviated as Filipino vegetable soup, is a Filipino vegetable soup made with green leafy vegetables and a variety of other vegetables in a broth seasoned with seafood or meat broth.

Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is Filipino's favorite pork dish cooked in sour tamarind soup. It is the ultimate comfort dish. Sinigang has been and will always be a staple of Filipino cuisine across the country.

Sinaing na Tulingan

Sinaing na Tulingan is a Filipino fish recipe that makes braised fish. The recipe uses bullet tuna. Tuna is simmered in a gentle clay pot or saucepan until the meat and bones are tender and infused with a delicate aroma.
