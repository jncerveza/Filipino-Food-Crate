Pastillas

History

Pitchi-pitchi originated from Quezon City, it is a traditional Filipino dessert made with freshly grated cassava, which is steamed and then topped with freshly grated coconut. It's usually shaped like a circle and made out of cassava and pandan extract/essence, topped with grated coconut meat. Pitchi-pitchi is pronounced pitsi-pitsi by Filipinos. It's a color orange rice based dessert.


https://www.kawalingpinoy.com/pastillas-de-leche/

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups grated cassava
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 pandan leaves
  • 2 cups water
  • ½ tsp lye water (lihia)

Procedure

  1. First, prepare the cassava. Peel off its skin then wash with water.

  2. Grate the cassava then set aside.

  3. Next, boil the pandan leaves in 2 cups of water until reduced to 1 cup.

  4. In a mixing bowl, pour the boiled water with pandan. Add the sugar then stir well.

  5. Add the food color and lihia then stir well.

  6. Add the grated cassava then mix well.
