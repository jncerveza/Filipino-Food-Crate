Rice Based

Sapin-sapin

Sapin-sapin, a layered glutonious rice and coconut dessert in the Philippines. It is made from rice flour, coconut milk, sugar, water, flavoring and coloring. Finalizing it by toping it with latik or toasted desiccated coconut flakes.

Suman

Suman also called budbud a rice cake originatedin the Philippines. It is made by glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk, often wrapped in banana leaves, coconut leaves, or buli or buri palm (Corypha) leaves for steaming. It is topped with sugar or laden with latik.

Pichi-pichi

Pichi-pichi, a gelatinous dessert that can also be eaten as a heavy mid-afternoon snack. It is made from grated cassava and sugar. It is coated with grated coconut for additional flavor.

Puto

Puto is a Filipino steamed rice cake made traditionally from slightly fermented rice dough (galapong). It is made from rice soaked overnight to allow it to ferment slightly. Puto also has various kinds of indigenous steamed cakes.

Palitaw

Palitaw, a small, flat, sweet rice cake eaten in the Philippines. It is made from galapong - washed, soaked, and ground malagkit (sticky rice). It can be topped with various toppings.
