Baked Goods

Pandesal

Pandesal is one of the most famous bread in the Philippines because of its softness. It is made by rolling the dough into long logs straight to fine bread crumbs. It can be paired with hot chocolates or coffees of your choice.

Mamon

Mamon is a soft, buttery, and fluffy chiffon or sponge cake. Best snacks for kids or even among adults.

Ensaymada

Every bakery in the Philippines sells ensaymada because it is so delicious. It has very rich dough with layers of butter and cheese.

Spanish Bread

Spanish bread is the Favorite ‘mirienda’ or afternoon snacks of Filipinos because of its multi layered flavor. It is rolled into a log enclosing within a sugary and buttery filling, and then rolled into breadcrumbs before baking. Perfect for afternoon snack.

Hopia

Another favourite afternoon snack of Filipinos is Hopia fillied with soft beans inside. It is made of thin flaky pastry, traditionally filled with mung bean paste (Munggo). There are more variation of flavor such as Ube or purple yam and the preferred flavour of Filipinos.

Otap

Otap is a food common in Cebu where it is certainly originated. It is an oval-shapped puff pastry cookie. The pastry must undergo 11 baking stages to achieve the texture.

Piaya

Piaya is a food common in Negros Occidental where it is a popular food because of its deliciousness. It is a muscuvado-filled unleavened flatbread. It is made by filling dough with a mixture of muscovado and glucose syrup.

Buko Pie

Buko pie is a traditional pie in the Philippines made with coconut. It has a flaky pie crust and a smooth and sweet filling when you eat it.

Egg Pie

Egg pie is a sweet and creamy egg pie that can be eaten as a snack or dessert and is usually made with flour, milk, egg, and sugar.

Puto Bumbong

Puto Bumbong is a famous dish that Filipino likes and it is mostly sold on the upcoming christmas. It is type of Filipino purple rice cake which is prepared by steaming ground purple rice mixture inside a bamboo tube. It is topped with margarine (or butter), freshly grated coconut, and muscovado sugar.
