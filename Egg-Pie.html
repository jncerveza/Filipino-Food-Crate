Egg Pie

History

Egg pie is a Filipino dessert or can be a snack. One of the popular food in the Philippines and can find in any bakery or known as “panaderya” here in the Philippines. Egg pie is almost the same as egg custard but has a difference in making it.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk, or 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 cup fresh or evaporated milk
  • 1 medium-sized egg yolk
  • 4 medium-sized eggs
  • 3/4 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh or evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 medium-sized egg white
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar

Procedure

    HOW TO MAKE FILIPINO EGG PIE

  1. To make the pie crust, in a large bowl, combine and mix the flour, condensed milk, evaporated milk, egg yolk, and butter until you form a dough. (Save this egg white for use in the pie filling later on.)

  2. Knead the dough until soft and tacky to touch. Add some more flour and milk if needed.

  3. Once the dough is soft to touch, cover it with cling wrap and rest for about 30 minutes to form gluten.

  4. Make the pie filling: In a large bowl, combine eggs, condensed milk, and vanilla. Whisk until slightly foamy. Set aside.

  5. Combine evaporated or fresh milk and condensed milk in a saucepan. Warm it up over low heat.

  6. Add the warmed milk mixture to the batter mixture and whisk again for another 2 to 3 minutes or until foamy.

  7. Make the meringue: in a separate bowl, combine egg whites and sugar, and whip egg whites to soft peaks. Gently mix egg whites into batter. Stir again for 3 minutes. Set aside.

  8. Form your dough according to the shape of your baking pan. Use a rolling pin or clean hands to firmly press the dough into the bottom and sides of it. Decorate the edges as desired. Once it's done, pour in the pie filling.

  9. Bake in a preheated oven (350 degrees F or 180 degrees C) for 50 minutes. Tip: 15 minutes before the time, lower the temperature of the oven to 320 degrees F or 160 degrees C to avoid burning the filling.
