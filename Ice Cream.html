Dirty Ice Cream

History

For it being a street food “sorbetes” is usually called “Dirty Ice Cream. In the 19th century Filipinos found out that carabao milk is cheap and alot of people can buy ice creams that are made from this source. These ice creams were traditionally hand made or churned by Filipino families, usually Filipinos use churners that are made of a bucket and a hand crank. Time passed by, sorbetes became much easier and cheaper to do. Instead of making it from carabao milk we found out that coconut milk can also be used. We also made varieties of flavors from queso (or cheese) to tropical mangoes. Sorbetes is now usually sold on a pull cart and served by what we call sorbetero, it's also served on a bun, a plastic cup or a wafer cone. Also one of the reasons why sorbetes is called dirty ice cream is because our Pinoy parents used to tell some of us that they washed their utensils and container with dirty water or didn't wash it at all.

Ingredients

  • 1⁄2 gallon powdered milk
  • 1 1⁄2 gallons coconut milk
  • 1⁄3 gallon evaporated milk
  • 2 kg sugar
  • 1⁄3 kg cornstarc

Procedure

  1. Mix all ingredients using mixer (or egg beater) until the consistency is foamy.

  2. Pour in a stainless steel pan or boiler. Let boil for 15 minutes to kill any microbes or bacteria. Set aside to cool.

  3. Put into freezer for half an hour or until half-frozen.

  4. Remove from freezer and mix again (with big wooden spon or egg beater) until the consistency becomes a thick mass.
