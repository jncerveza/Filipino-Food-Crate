Seafood Dishes

Daing

Daing na Isda is a type of fried fish in which the fish is separated, gutted, seasoned, and sun-dried before frying. It can be eaten for breakfast, lunch and dinner.

Escabeche

Escabeche is usually fish or meat, marinated and/or cooked in vinegar and a little spice. However, in the Philippines, the fish is first fried and then dipped in sweet and sour sauce, or marinated sweet and sour pork.

Inihaw na Bangus

Inihaw na Bangus means grilled milk fish. It is a very delicious tangy dish that anyone can easily prepare. It pairs well with soy sauce dip and rice. It is stuffed with onions, tomatoes, ginger, salt and pepper.

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is 'cooked' without the application of heat but is rather cured in vinegar. Kinilaw recipes are easy to prepare and are typically used for casual encounters. It is a popular "pulutan" or food served during casual drinking. But it also makes a great snack.

Paksiw

Paksiw is a Filipino style dish that means "cooked in vinegar and stewed". Paksiw na Isda is where the fish is boiled in vinegar and seasoned with fish sauce and chili.

Pritong Tilapia

Tilapia is the all time favorite dish. It is a versatile fish as it can be fried, grilled, braised and grilled. Since it has a mild flavor, it is ideal for sweet and sour dishes and other marinades.

Sarciado

Sarciadong Isda or Fish Sarciado translates as "Fish Sauce". Fried fish cooked in a sauce that usually includes tomatoes and onions; Some spices are also added to enhance the taste.

Sinaing na Tulingan

Sinaing na Tulingan is a Filipino fish recipe that makes braised fish. The recipe uses bullet tuna. Tuna is simmered in a gentle clay pot or saucepan until the meat and bones are tender and infused with a delicate aroma.

Tuyo

Tuyo fish is a type of dried fish from the Philippines. This dish is usually fried quickly and served with rice and spicy vinegar dip. Tuyo is usually eaten for breakfast in the Philippines, although this dried fish can also be eaten at any time of the day.

Ukoy

Ukoy is the Filipino version of shrimp fritters. Small shrimp with head and shell are mixed into batter and fried until crispy. It can be an appetizer, main course or mid-afternoon snack.
