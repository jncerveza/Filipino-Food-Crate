Law-uy

History

Law-uy is a famous vegetable soup meal in Mindanao; at first glance, it resembles dinengdeng or laswa, but this dish is rather distinct, and it appears to have a lot of slimy vegetables and root crops. The key vegetables that give it its distinct consistency are jute leaves (Saluyot), okra, loofah (Patola), and taro. Squash, eggplant, and moringa leaves (malunggay) are added after which it is flavored with lemongrass, fatty pig, and/or fried fish.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 lb. squash
  • 2 pieces taro
  • 1 piece Knorr Shrimp Cube
  • 4 thumbs ginger
  • 2 stalks lemongrass
  • 1 piece Chinese eggplant sliced
  • 10 pieces string beans cut in 2 inch pieces
  • 12 pieces okra
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 3 1/2 cups water
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Procedure

  1. Boil water in a cooking pot. Add lemongrass and ginger. Cook for 5 minutes.

  2. Add taro and squash. Cover and continue boiling for 5 to 7 minutes.

  3. Remove lemongrass from the pot. Add okra, string beans, and eggplant. Stir. Cover and continue cooking for 7 minutes.

  4. Add Knorr Shrimp Cube. Stir and cook for 3 minutes.

  5. Add spinach. Cook for 2 minutes.

  6. Season with salt and ground black pepper.

  7. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.

  8. Share and Enjoy!
